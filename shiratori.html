Shiritori Game

Shiritori (尻取り) is a word play game that involves saying Japanese words back and forth.
The catch is the next word must start with the same kana as the previously words’ last kana.
The word shiritori is a shortening of shiri o toru (尻を取る) with the two kanji for “rear” and “take”,
so the word essentially means “to take the rear”.

Rules:

  • Solo player.
  • A player who writes a word ending with the syllable N (ん) loses.
  • Words should be contain more than 4 letters.
  • Words should not be repeated.
  • The next word must start with the last letter of the previous word.
